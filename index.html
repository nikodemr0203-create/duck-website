Welcome to Duck Species Guide

Ducks are fascinating waterfowl found on every continent except Antarctica. They are highly adaptable birds that thrive in various aquatic environments. This guide provides educational information about different duck species from around the world.

Duck Species

Mallard

Scientific Name: Anas platyrhynchos

Habitat: Northern Hemisphere wetlands, lakes, and rivers

Description: Mallards are one of the most recognizable duck species. Males have a distinctive green head, yellow bill, and white neck ring, while females are mottled brown. They are dabbling ducks and typically feed by tipping forward in shallow water.

Diet: Seeds, plants, insects, and small aquatic animals

Size: 50-65 cm in length

Wood Duck

Scientific Name: Aix sponsa

Habitat: Eastern North America, woodland streams, and swamps

Description: Wood ducks are among the most colorful duck species in North America. Males have iridescent green heads, chestnut breasts, and distinctive patterns. They are excellent fliers and are the only North American duck that typically raises two broods per year.

Diet: Acorns, seeds, berries, and aquatic plants

Size: 47-54 cm in length

Pekin Duck

Scientific Name: Anas platyrhynchos domesticus

Habitat: Domesticated breed, farms and gardens worldwide

Description: Pekin ducks are a domesticated breed developed in China. They are characterized by their white plumage, orange bill and feet, and upright posture. They are heavier than wild ducks and excellent layers of large eggs.

Diet: Grains, vegetables, insects, and aquatic plants

Size: 60-70 cm in length

Teal (Green-winged Teal)

Scientific Name: Anas crecca

Habitat: Northern temperate regions, shallow wetlands

Description: Green-winged teals are among the smallest dabbling ducks. Males have a chestnut head with a green eye patch, while females are mottled gray-brown. They are swift fliers and highly migratory.

Diet: Seeds, aquatic plants, and small invertebrates

Size: 34-38 cm in length

Muscovy Duck

Scientific Name: Cairina moschata

Habitat: Tropical South America, now domesticated worldwide

Description: Muscovy ducks are distinct from other domestic ducks. Males are larger and have dark plumage with a red facial mask covered in caruncles (fleshy growths). They are strong fliers and can roost in trees.

Diet: Seeds, grains, insects, and aquatic vegetation

Size: Males 66-81 cm, females 50-61 cm

Mandarin Duck

Scientific Name: Aix galericulata

Habitat: East Asia, woodland streams and parks

Description: Mandarin ducks are among the most beautiful duck species. Males display striking colors with orange, blue, green, and purple plumage arranged in unique patterns. They are cavity nesters and prefer wooded areas near water.

Diet: Seeds, acorns, plants, and aquatic invertebrates

Size: 41-49 cm in length

Fun Duck Facts

  • Ducks have waterproof feathers due to natural oils secreted from a gland near their tail
  • A duck's quack doesn't produce an echo - scientists are still unsure why
  • Ducks can sleep with one eye open and half their brain awake to watch for predators
  • Male ducks are called drakes, females are called hens, and babies are called ducklings
  • Some duck species can fly at speeds over 60 mph
  • Ducks have excellent vision and can see ultraviolet light
  • A duck's webbed feet have no nerves or blood vessels, so they don't feel cold
  • Ducks have been domesticated for over 4,000 years
